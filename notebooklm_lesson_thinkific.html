Using NotebookLM in Your Classroom

NotebookLM is a Google AI tool that lets you upload your own documents, then chat with an AI that is grounded exclusively in those materials. Unlike ChatGPT or Gemini, it doesn't draw on general internet knowledge — it only knows what you give it.

Key idea: NotebookLM is a source-grounded AI. Answers are tied directly to your documents, which means fewer hallucinations and more reliable results for classroom use.

Things you can upload:

Google Docs PDFs Slides YouTube links Web URLs Audio files Text files

Getting started is fast. Here's the process from your first login to your first conversation:

01
Go to notebooklm.google.com
Sign in with your Google account. Free accounts get up to 50 notebooks with 50 sources each.
02
Create a new notebook
Click "New Notebook." Name it after your unit or topic.
03
Add your sources
Upload PDFs, paste a URL, or link a YouTube video. Multiple sources at once is fine.
04
Start chatting
Use the chat panel on the right to ask questions, get summaries, or build study tools.
Pro tip: After uploading, check the Notebook Guide panel. NotebookLM auto-generates a summary, key topics, and suggested questions instantly.

NotebookLM won't make things up — but being specific with your prompts still matters a lot. The more context you give, the more useful the response.

NotebookLM always cites the source it used. Click any citation to see the exact passage. This is a powerful verification habit to model for students.

Prompts that work well:

"Summarize the main argument of this article in 3 sentences."
"Create 5 discussion questions based on chapter 3."
"What evidence in these sources supports the claim that ___?"
"Rewrite this passage for a 6th grade reading level."
"Give me a vocabulary list with definitions from this document."

Before bringing any AI tool into your classroom, understand its limitations and your responsibilities around student data.

!
No student PII
Do not upload documents with student names, IDs, or identifiable data. Use curriculum materials only.
!
50 source limit
Each notebook holds up to 50 sources. Organize by unit, not by full course.
~
Audio is experimental
The Audio Overview feature is impressive but can be imprecise. Preview before sharing with students.
✓
Free to use
NotebookLM is free with a Google account. No subscription needed to get started.
