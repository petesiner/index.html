Open Gemini & explore the side panel
▾
Get familiar with where Gemini lives and what the panel offers.
  • Open any document in Google Docs
  • Click Ask Gemini at the top right to open the side panel
  • Browse the suggested prompts — click More suggestions to see additional options
  • Locate the Expand, Collapse, and Close controls at the top of the panel
💡 The panel remembers your conversation — but history clears when you refresh or close the doc. Insert important outputs before leaving.
✓ Gemini panel open and explored
02
📋
Summarize a document
▾
Use Gemini to get a quick read on a document without reading every word.
  • Open a document that has text content — a lesson plan, reading, or article works well
  • In the Gemini panel, try one of the example prompts below
  • Highlight a specific paragraph, then ask Gemini to summarize just that section
  • Click Insert to add the summary to your document, or Retry for a different version
Try these prompts
Summarize this document in 3 bullet points.
Explain this document like I'm 5 years old.
✓ Document summarized with Gemini
03
✏️
Edit & refine content
▾
Use the Refine tool to rewrite, adjust tone, or strengthen a section of your writing.
  • Select a paragraph of text in your document
  • On the floating bar that appears, click Refine
  • Try at least two options: Rephrase, Enhance, Shorten, More formal, or More casual
  • Review the suggested changes, then click Accept all or Reject all
Or enter a custom prompt
Add evidence to my Research section.
Suggest places where I can better substantiate my argument.
💡 If you paste new content into an existing doc, use Match writing style to blend it seamlessly.
✓ Content refined with Gemini
04
🖼️
Generate an image
▾
Create a custom image for your document without leaving Docs.
  • In the Gemini side panel, type a prompt describing an image you want
  • Hover over a generated image and click Insert to add it to your doc
  • If you want variations, click Generate more for similar images
  • If the result isn't right, click Edit prompt to adjust your description and try again
Example image prompts
Create an image of a classroom with students collaborating.
Generate a simple diagram showing the writing process.
✓ Image generated and inserted
05
📂
Reference Drive files & search the web
▾
Pull in context from your own files or the web to strengthen your content.
  • In the bottom bar, click Sources → Add from Drive to attach a file
  • Or type @ in the prompt box and search for a file from your Drive
  • Try a web-search prompt using the phrase "using web search" to pull in current information
  • Review the sources Gemini used by clicking Source in the response
Try these prompts
Update the project timeline based on the latest updates from our meeting notes doc.
Write a paragraph about current research on student engagement using web search.
💡 Gemini can pull from Drive files, Gmail, or the web — you choose which sources to allow.
